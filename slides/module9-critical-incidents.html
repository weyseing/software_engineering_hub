The Challenge

When Production Goes Down

The manager's role is not to fix the code — it is to create the conditions for the team to fix it fast, with full accountability to stakeholders.

P1 · Revenue

Payment Outage

Transaction failures at checkout. Every minute of downtime is direct revenue loss and merchant churn risk across the platform.

P1 · Security

Auth / IAM Failure

RBAC misconfiguration causing access denial or unintended privilege escalation across merchant and admin portals.

P1 · Stability

Deploy Regression

Bad GitLab pipeline promotion to production. Service degraded, rollback blocked by an in-flight data migration.

Detected via Datadog APM alerts and New Relic synthetic monitors. All P1 incidents immediately trigger war room protocol.
Framework

War Room — Roles & Ownership

Clear role assignment within the first two minutes eliminates confusion under pressure. Everyone has exactly one job.

Incident Commander

Engineering Manager

Owns the timeline, decisions, and escalation path. Does not debug. Keeps the team unblocked and communicates on a strict 10-minute cadence to all stakeholders.

Technical Lead

Senior / Staff Engineer

Drives root cause analysis and owns the technical fix. Deploys via GitLab pipeline. Reports status to Incident Commander at every decision point.

Comms Lead

EM or Senior Engineer

Single source of truth for product, operations, and leadership. Owns Slack updates and status page. Never contradicts the Incident Commander.

Scribe

Any Assigned Engineer

Logs every action and exact timestamp in real time. This document is the raw input for the post-mortem. No detail is too small to capture.

Execution

Incident Response Timeline

Four disciplined phases from first alert to full resolution — with clear ownership and time-boxed decision gates at each stage.

T + 0

Detect & Declare

Datadog alert fires. EM declares incident severity (P1/P2). War room channel opened. Roles assigned. Scribe begins logging immediately.

T + 5

Triage & Contain

Blast radius identified. Affected service isolated. Circuit breaker or traffic shift applied where possible. First stakeholder update sent.

T + 20

Diagnose & Fix

Root cause identified via Datadog APM and New Relic traces. Fix deployed through GitLab with emergency approval gate. Canary validated before full rollout.

T + 35

Verify & Close

Full traffic restored. SLO recovery confirmed in Datadog. All-clear communicated to stakeholders. Post-mortem scheduled within 24 hours.

Learning & Prevention

Blameless Post-Mortem

The incident is only half the work. The post-mortem converts a production failure into a structural, permanent improvement to the system.

Step 01

What Happened

Timestamped event reconstruction. No blame. Focus exclusively on system and process failures — not individual mistakes or judgement calls made under pressure.

Step 02

Root Cause Analysis

5 Whys or fishbone analysis. Distinguish the proximate trigger from the underlying systemic cause that allowed the failure to reach production.

Step 03

Action Items

Assigned owners, firm deadlines, tracked as GitLab issues. Progress reviewed and reported in the following sprint retrospective without exception.

Principle: Every incident is a signal — it reveals exactly where the system and process need to improve. Treat it as an investment, not a cost.
